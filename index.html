    Intermediate

    Intermediate refers to a level of proficiency or skill that falls between beginner and advanced. It is a term commonly used in education and training to describe a stage in a learning process. At the intermediate level, learners typically have a solid foundation but still need to develop and refine their skills.

    Degree

    A degree is an academic credential awarded by a college or university to students who have successfully completed a program of study. The most common types of degrees are bachelor's, master's, and doctoral degrees. Earning a degree typically requires a significant investment of time, effort, and money, but it can lead to greater career opportunities and higher earning potential. The type of degree and field of study can vary widely, from liberal arts and sciences to engineering and business.

    Post Graduation

    Post Graduation, also known as a graduate degree, is a program of study that is pursued after completing a bachelor's degree. It typically involves more specialized and advanced coursework and may include research or thesis requirements. Examples of post-graduate degrees include master's degrees, doctoral degrees, and professional degrees such as law or medicine. Pursuing a post-graduate degree can provide opportunities for career advancement and higher salaries, as well as the development of advanced knowledge and skills in a particular field.
